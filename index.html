Hi, I am Madelon Hulsebos, a PhD student at the University of Amsterdam and intern at Sigma Computing.
I am interested in table representations and models, and their applications in data management like data integration, search and validation.

In 2018, at the MIT Media Lab, I got the opportunity to develop Sherlock, a deep learning model aimed at accurately inferring table semantics at scale. The massive interest from industry in Sherlock inspired me to start a PhD (2020) at the INDE Lab (UvA) to focus on improving table models and their applicability in practice. A piece of this puzzle is GitTables: a large-scale dataset of tables extracted from CSV files on GitHub, enriched with table semantics.

I helped organizing the SemTab '21 challenge, and reviewed for NeurIPS '21 (Datasets & Benchmarks track), WWW '22 (Industry track), and EDBT '22 (Industry track). Besides academia, I am member of the supervisory board of a student consulting firm and was a data scientist for 2+ years.
