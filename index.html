Hi, I am Madelon Hulsebos, a PhD student at the University of Amsterdam and intern at Sigma Computing. I am interested in systems that capture table semantics using machine learning and their applications in data management like data integration, search and validation.

In 2018, at the MIT Media Lab, I got the opportunity to develop Sherlock, a deep learning model aimed at accurately inferring table semantics at scale. The massive interest from industry in Sherlock inspired me to start a PhD (2020) at the INDE Lab (UvA) to focus on improving semantic table models and their applicability in practice. A piece to this puzzle is GitTables, a large-scale dataset of tables extracted from GitHub and enriched with table semantics.

I reviewed for NeurIPS '21 (Datasets & Benchmarks track), WWW '22 (Industry track), and EDBT '22 (Industry track), and served in the organizing committee of SemTab '21. Besides academia, I am member of the supervisory board of a student consulting firm and was a data scientist for over 2 years.
