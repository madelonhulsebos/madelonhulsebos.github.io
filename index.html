About me

I'm a postdoctoral fellow at UC Berkeley, member of ELLIS, and incoming faculty at CWI Amsterdam. I obtained my PhD at the University of Amsterdam with research at Sigma Computing and the MIT Media Lab. Before academia, I was a data scientist for 2+ years, working on automating ML-driven data analysis pipelines.

I believe that tables are a promising modality for representation learning and generative models (e.g. LLMs): tables are everywhere and drive many high-value applications in organizations, from enterprises to governments. Therefore, I research Table Representation Learning (TRL) with the overall objective to make insight retrieval from structured data a walk in the park for everyone. Some of my research on this can be found below, but I also maintain an index of TRL research in this emerging research area!

To drive research around TRL and develop a new community, I founded the Table Representation Learning workshop series at NeurIPS in 2022. I organize various related efforts such as the TRL Dagstuhl Seminar and the Data Management for E2E ML workshop at SIGMOD, and review for various tracks/workshops at e.g. VLDB, SIGMOD, NeurIPS, ICLR, WWW.

Are you interested in a PhD around TRL and applications in beautiful Amsterdam? I'll open 4-5 PhD positions and 1 postdoc position in 2025 and 2026, leave your info here if you are interested! I'm also seeking strong industry partners for collaboration, please reach out if your team is interested!
