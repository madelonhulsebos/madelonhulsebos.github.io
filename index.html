About me

I'm a faculty at CWI in Amsterdam and member of ELLIS. Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at UC Berkeley and obtained my PhD at the University of Amsterdam with research at Sigma Computing and MIT. Before academia, I spent 2+ years in industry working on automating ML-driven data analysis pipelines.

My mission is to establish tabular data as a modality for representation learning and generative models (e.g. LLMs), since tables are everywhere, store valuable information, and drive many high-value applications in organizations from enterprises to governments and healthcare. Therefore, I research Table Representation Learning (TRL) with the overall objective to make insight retrieval from structured data a walk in the park for everyone. Some of my research on this can be found below, but I also maintain an index of TRL research in this emerging research area!

To drive research around TRL and foster a strong community, I lead the Table Representation Learning initiative at the ELLIS unit Amsterdam and founded the Table Representation Learning workshop series at NeurIPS and ACL since 2022. I organize various related efforts around TRL at Dagstuhl and beyond, and review for various tracks/workshops at e.g. VLDB, SIGMOD, NeurIPS, ICLR, WWW.
