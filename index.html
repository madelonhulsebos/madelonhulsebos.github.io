About me

I'm a faculty at CWI where I lead the Table Representation Learning (TRL) Lab and am member of the Database Architectures group. I'm also an ELLIS faculty with the Amsterdam unit. Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at UC Berkeley and obtained my PhD at the University of Amsterdam with research at Sigma Computing and MIT. Before academia, I spent 2+ years in industry working on automating data analysis pipelines with ML.

Since 2020, I'm on a mission to establish tabular data as a key modality for representation learning and generative models (e.g. LLMs), because tables are everywhere, contain valuable data, and drive important decision-making processes in organizations such as governments, NGOs, enterprises, and hospitals. Therefore, I research Table Representation Learning (TRL) with the objective to democratize insights from structured data.

To drive research around TRL and foster a strong community, I initiated the Table Representation Learning theme at the ELLIS unit Amsterdam and founded the Table Representation Learning workshop series at NeurIPS and ACL since 2022. I organize various related efforts around TRL at Dagstuhl and beyond, and review for various tracks/workshops at e.g. VLDB, SIGMOD, NeurIPS, ICLR, WWW.

Research interests
I'm generally interested in Table Representation Learning (TRL), with a particular focus on:

  • (Relational) Table Embeddings
  • Generative Models (LLMs) for Tabular Data, e.g. for question answering, data wrangling, etc
  • Retrieval over Tables (data lakes, relational databases)
  • End-to-end (Agentic) Data Analysis and Data Science over Tabular Data
